Locke-Wallace Marital Adjustment Test (MAT)18
Details:
  Institution:REDLOC
  Contact:Brenda Minor
  Contact email:brenda.l.minor@vumc.org
  Submitted by:Brenda Minor
  Description:The Locke-Wallace Short Marital-Adjustment Test is a 15 item instrument, which as the test name would suggest, purports to measure marital adjustment. The authors define marital adjustment as “accommodation of a husband and wife to each other at a given time.” The instrument first requests the examinee’s general impression of marriage happiness on a continuum from “Very Unhappy” to “Perfectly Happy”. Then, additional specific statements and questions are given to which the examinee is asked to respond.
  Acknowledgement:Locke, H., & Wallace, K. (1959). Short marital-adjustment and prediction tests: Their reliability and validity. Marriage and Family Living, 21, 251-255.

  Description:The Lower Extremity Functional Scale (LEFS) is a questionnaire containing 20 questions about a person’s ability to perform everyday tasks. The LEFS can be used by clinicians as a measure of patients' initial function, ongoing progress and outcome, as well as to set functional goals. The LEFS can be used to evaluate the functional impairment of a patient with a disorder of one or both lower extremities. It can be used to monitor the patient over time and to evaluate the effectiveness of an intervention.
